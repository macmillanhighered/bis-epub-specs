Extended description for Map 1.3 Greece and the Aegean Sea, 1500 B.C.E.

The map shows Greece with the Ionian Sea to the west, the Mediterranean Sea to the south, and the Aegean Sea to the east. The region of Anatolia lies east of the Aegean Sea.

The Mycenean civilization encompasses southern Greece and the Cyclades Islands and other islands in the Aegean Sea. This area consists of the Peloponnese, with the city-states of Pylos to the west, Mycenae in the center, Gla to the northeast, and Athens to the east. The Cyclades Islands host the city of Thera.

The Minoan civilization encompasses the island of Crete to the southeast of Greece in the Mediterranean Sea.

A distance key indicates that the region of Mycenean civilzation was roughly 100 miles east-to-west and 200 miles north-to-south. The Minoan region is about 100 miles east-to-west and less than 50 miles north-to-south.
