Extended description for Figure 6.1: The human cortex and that of some other species

The first illustration shows a Rat brain. A small blue structure at the front of the brain is labeled Olfactory bulb.

The second illustration shows a slightly larger Cat brain. A small blue structure at the front of the brain is labeled Olfactory bulb.

The third illustration shows a larger Chimpanzee brain. The Frontal lobe takes up about half of the front of the brain and is shaded red.

The fourth illustration shows the Human brain which is the largest. The frontal lobe takes up about half of the front of the brain and is shaded red. The rear of the brain is shaded green and labeled Parietal lobe. Between the frontal lobe and the parietal lobe is an area that is shaded blue and labeled Motor cortex. At the back of the brain, the Occipital lobe is shaded yellow and the Visual cortex is shaded purple. At the bottom of the brain is a light blue area that is labeled Temporal lobe.
